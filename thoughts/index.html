tracking

I have decided to add tracking to my website for two reasons:

  1. plausible is a tool I 100% identify with
  2. ethical demonstration of an unethical issue

Some don‘t know and some don‘t care about tracking on the web – this is a problem.

I have installed a tool called plausible onto my website, which allows me to track every request to my site.
plausible.io an open-source tracking software. It does not keep any personal information and therefore anonymizes all gathered information. Of course, this is only pseudo-anonynomous to a certain degree. If my website does not get a lot of traffic and I send you a link to visit my site and I know you are using a specific operating system and reside in a specific country I can filter and make assumptions in the dashboard about who you might be.

I encourage you to visit the dashboard I have made publicly available (I am not tracking who visits it ;-)). You will see all the gathered information:

  • Number of unique visitors
  • Number of page views
  • Percentage of bounce rate (visitor only looking at one site)
  • Average time spent on website
  • Where did the visitor come from
  • What pages have been visited the most
  • What country is the visitor from
  • What device, operating system, and browser was used

This is only a subset of the information a tool could extract from a visit. The possibilites for tracking on the web is endless. In the how does tracking work? section I explain how tracking works and why it is even possible.
